{% trans "About Validibot" %}

Validibot is developed by McQuillen Interactive, a Melbourne-based web application studio founded by Daniel McQuillen. I've been building practical software for research labs, building scientists, and educators for more than two decades.

The idea for Validibot first took shape while creating a glazing system validation tool for the Windows and Daylighting Group at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. Watching that tool save engineers hours of back-and-forth convinced me that smart, simple validation workflows could help any team that wrestles with complex data submissions.

{% trans "What I'm Building" %}

My goal is to give product teams, analysts, and domain experts a dependable service that makes data quality checks feel routine and helpful instead of tedious and draining.

Validibot blends automated validation rules {% if ENABLE_AI_VALIDATIONS %} , AI-assisted review, {% endif %} and clear audit trails. It hides complex logic or custom simulations behind a simple interface, so you and your users can validate data with confidence and ease.

{% trans "Why It Matters" %}

Whether I am helping a building energy-efficiency researcher, a nonprofit educator, or a commercial product team, the same themes surface: people want software that respects their time, is easy to use, and keeps stakeholders aligned. Validibot carries that ethos forward, allowing authors to craft clear and orderly workflows so teams can validate and trust their data, respond faster, and focus on what matters most to their organization.

{% trans "What's Next" %}

I'm working with a select group of early users to make sure the system provides value and fits naturally into their workflows. If this all sounds interesting, I would love to hear how Validibot can support you.
